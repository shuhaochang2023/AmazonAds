The Parent Discovery
Focus on the relatable parent struggle — you've been searching for a fish oil your picky kid will actually take. This is your honest discovery moment.
0:00 – 0:08Opening Hook
Start holding or receiving the package. Set the scene — you're a parent who's been trying to find a fish oil your kid will actually take.

Open with something relatable about picky eaters or the struggle of getting nutrients into a child who refuses vegetables. Your audience is the parent who worries their 3-to-5-year-old isn't getting enough DHA for their growing brain.

Speak directly to that feeling. Keep it casual, like talking to a friend.
0:08 – 0:25The Unboxing
Open the package on camera. Take out the Daiken box — show the colorful kid-friendly packaging naturally (both sides, Chinese text included).

Key moments:
• Hold the box up — point out "Sugar Free," "Norwegian," "High Absorption" on the packaging
• Pull out the blister packs — this is a key moment: each gummy is individually sealed in foil, keeping it fresh and preventing oxidation (unlike a bottle where gummies sit in air)
• Pop one gummy out — show the bright orange color, soft chew texture, small enough for a child
0:25 – 0:40Why This One Is Different (Concentration + Absorption)
This is where you set Daiken apart. Explain WHY it matters in plain language.

Lead with concentration:
"You know those big cheap bottles of kids' fish oil? Check the label — most only have 100 to 150mg of omega-3 per gummy. This one has 300mg in a SINGLE gummy. That's why this box is smaller — you don't need to give your kid a handful."
Absorption: "And it's rTG form from Norway — the premium form that actually gets absorbed. Most cheaper brands use a form your body can't absorb as well."

Vitamin D bonus: "It also has Vitamin D3 built in — most fish oil gummies don't include that at all."

Smell test: Smell the gummy on camera. React honestly — confirm no fishy smell. Mention it's zero sugar, sweetened naturally.

If your child is present, let them see or smell it — genuine reactions are powerful.
0:40 – 0:52The Kid Test / Taste Moment

"Yes, there are bigger, cheaper bottles out there — but check what you're actually getting per gummy. Most are low-concentration. With Daiken, one gummy gives your kid 300mg omega-3 in a form their body actually absorbs. You're not wasting gummies."

SAFE LANGUAGE (use these)

✗ "This will cure..." or "This treats..."
✗ "Clinically proven to make kids smarter"
✗ "Your child NEEDS this" or any fear-based urgency
✗ Any specific disease or medical condition claims
✗ Any mention of competitor brand names

If your video contains medical/health cure claims, we will request a re-edit or cut those segments in post-production. Keep language to "supports" and "helps with" — never "cures," "treats," or "prevents."

DO's
✓Be yourself — authentic, warm, parent-to-parent energy
✓Show the product clearly (box front & back, blister packs, gummy close-up)
✓Do the smell test on camera — no fishy smell is a key differentiator
✓Explain the rTG absorption advantage in simple terms
✓Lead with concentration: 300mg per ONE gummy vs competitors' low-dose big bottles
✓Show packaging naturally as-is, including Chinese text
✓Include your child's genuine reaction if possible
✓Mention the brand story (since 1961, quality awards)
✓Film in natural light, home setting (kitchen, living room)
✓End with a clear call to action
DON'Ts
✗Do NOT show or mention competitor products by name
✗Do NOT make medical claims ("cures," "treats," "prevents" any condition)
✗Do NOT rush — let viewers see and process the product
✗Do NOT use heavy or distracting background music
✗Do NOT over-explain science — keep rTG to 1–2 simple sentences
✗Do NOT hide or apologize for bilingual packaging
✗Do NOT script word-for-word — keep it natural and conversational
